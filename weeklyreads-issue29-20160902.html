WeeklyReads: Issue 29

Welcome to Issue 29. This week's list is lighter than usual but still has some dev tricks, a prototyping tool and a productivity/automation app for iOS.

WeeklyReads is an email list for design, development and much more. Brought to you by a human on the internet.
 



Design and Development


Fighting the space between inline-block elements.
Another great post from CSS Tricks.


CanvasFlip
Take your designs and create interactive, trackable prototypes. CanvasFlip will give you analytics on how many sessions used your prototype, how many people dropped off, how many people fulfilled the main actions expected, etc.


11 npm tricks
A bunch of these were a surprise to me and all of them are great tips. Definitely worth a quick read.


Motion Periodic Table
Dive into different types of easing and animations, accompanied by gifs. The text isn't in english, but you can get a good amount of info from the gifs alone.




Apps


Melodist for iOS
A new app that takes photos and creates custom melodies to represent them.


Pastebot for Mac, Beta
Tapbots, the team behind the best Twitter app around, have released a beta of a new clipboard management app. Check it out.


Candy for Chrome
A Chrome extension that helps you save links, highlights and more.


Duuoo
If you manage a team, you should probably be doing 1:1's. Duuoo promises to make 1:1's more effective, actionable and more.


Actions for iOS
Actions is an app the helps automate repetitive actions you take on your phone. Worth mentioning that this is similar to Workflow for iOS, which is very robust.


BaseTrip
Are you planning a trip soon? Use BaseTrip to see all the relevant information about your destination. Some of these data points include: weather, cost of living, electric socket type, transportation, crime and emergency contact info. This is a great resource.
